Privacy Policy — Morrow

Last updated: 6 July 2026

Morrow (“Morrow”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) is an AI reflection journal that helps you open your day with an intention and close it with a short reflection — the Daily Loop. This policy explains what data the app handles, how, and your choices.

Summary

  • No account. You do not sign up or log in. Your journal lives on your device.
  • Local-first. Entries are stored on your device (Apple SwiftData) and, if you enable it, synced across your own devices through your private iCloud account.
  • AI reflections. The text you write during a reflection is sent — through a lightweight relay we operate — to a third-party AI provider so it can respond and help shape your intention and goals. We do not sell your data or use it for advertising.
  • Voice stays on device. Voice input is transcribed to text on your device using Apple’s on-device speech recognition. Audio recordings are not uploaded to us.

Information we handle

Content you create. Your moods, intentions, goals, notes, and reflection text, stored locally on your device.

AI dialogue text. When you reflect with the AI, the text of that conversation for the current day only is transmitted to our relay and forwarded to a third-party AI provider to generate a response and extract your intention and goals. Previous days are not sent back into the conversation. Insights (mood trends, streaks) are computed on your device.

Voice input. Audio is captured only to transcribe it to text on your device via Apple’s Speech framework; the audio itself is not sent to us.

Subscription status. Morrow is a paid subscription with a trial. Purchases are processed by Apple via the App Store; we receive whether your subscription is active and do not receive your payment card details.

Diagnostic data. If you share diagnostics with Apple, Apple may give us aggregated, non-identifying reports. We do not embed third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.

We do not collect your name, email, phone number, contacts, precise location, photos, or advertising identifiers.

How your information is used

  • To provide the core experience: saving your day, generating AI reflections, and showing local Insights.
  • To sync your entries across your own devices, if you enable iCloud.
  • To operate and secure our AI relay (basic rate-limiting and abuse prevention).

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your journal content for advertising.

Third parties

  • Apple — on-device storage, iCloud sync, on-device speech recognition, and App Store subscription processing (Apple Privacy Policy).
  • Our AI provider — receives your current-day reflection text to generate responses, under its own privacy terms.

Data retention and deletion

Entries remain on your device until you delete them or delete the app. With iCloud sync on, copies live in your private iCloud until removed. Reflection text sent to the AI provider may be retained transiently by that provider per its policy; we do not keep a separate server-side copy of your journal.

Your choices and rights

  • Turn iCloud sync on or off in your device settings.
  • Delete individual entries or the entire app at any time.
  • Under laws such as GDPR or CCPA/CPRA you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data. Because your journal is stored on your device and we keep no user accounts, you can exercise most of these rights directly on the device; otherwise contact us.

Children

Morrow is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age in your country) and is not intended for their use.

Not medical advice

Morrow is a journaling and reflection tool, not a medical device or a substitute for professional therapy, diagnosis, or treatment. If you are in crisis, contact local emergency services or a crisis line.

Changes

We may update this policy. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
